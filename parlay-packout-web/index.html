How this works

Every added leg is a moneyline lean from the Slate tab. Combined probability = each leg's win probability multiplied together (assumes independence, which is roughly true across different games). More legs = lower combined probability even if each individual pick looks solid — that's the mathematical trade-off of parlays, not a flaw in the research. A 3-leg parlay of "likely" picks might hit 35–45% of the time; a 10-leg parlay of the same quality picks might hit under 5%. Parlays are inherently -EV relative to straight bets in almost all cases because the book's hold compounds on every leg — use them for entertainment/upside, not as a primary strategy.
